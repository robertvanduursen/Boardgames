Questions & Answers

\n

\n
\n Can Anger be used on a friendly creature from a non-active house?\n\n
\n\n
\n
\n

Yes. The only restriction is that the creature you select is \xe2\x80\x9cfriendly\xe2\x80\x9d (i.e. under your control). Therefore, you can choose any friendly creature in play. That creature must fight, if possible.

\n What happens if Anger is used on a creature and there are no opposing creatures to fight?\n\n
\n\n
\n
\n

If the opponent has no creatures on their battleline, the creature is readied, but does not fight. Assuming the creature is usable \xe2\x80\x93 e.g. via the active house or another ability \xe2\x80\x93 you can then choose to use the creature in a different way, such as reap; otherwise, the creature is unable to be used.

\n\n

Example: if there are no enemy creatures in play and you choose Brobnar as your active house, you could reap with a Brobnar creature and play Anger to ready it. Since there are no enemy creatures to fight, you could choose to use the creature in another way, such as reaping again.

\n Can Anger be used on a friendly creature that is not exhausted?\n\n
\n\n
\n
\n

Yes. You always resolve as much of an ability as possible. Therefore, the creature would remain ready and then it would fight. If there are no enemy creatures to fight, the \xe2\x80\x9cPlay:\xe2\x80\x9d ability would not resolve, but you would still get the \xc3\x86mber bonus for playing Anger.

\n If I don\xe2\x80\x99t have any creatures in play, may I still play Anger to gain the \xc3\x86mber bonus?\n\n
\n\n
\n
\n

Yes. The first step in playing any card is to receive the \xc3\x86mber bonus, if any. Therefore, you can always play action cards to get the \xc3\x86mber bonus, but are still obligated to resolve as much of the action card as possible. If the \xe2\x80\x9cPlay:\xe2\x80\x9d ability of the action card is not resolvable (e.g. there are no viable targets), you still keep the \xc3\x86mber bonus.

\n What happens if Anger is used on a stunned creature?\n\n
\n\n
\n
\n

The stunned creature would become exhausted and the stun token would be removed. No fight would take place, and no \xe2\x80\x9cFight:\xe2\x80\x9d abilities would resolve.

\n\n

Any attempt to fight with a stunned creature will invoke the normal stun removal rules, even if circumstances wouldn\xe2\x80\x99t normally allow the creature to fight, such as no opposing creatures or a card ability that prevents creatures from being used to fight. When a creature is stunned, the next time the creature is used (e.g. fight, reap, or another action) the only thing that happens is the creature exhausts and the stun token is removed. Whether you attempt to fight, reap, or perform another action, it does not resolve, nor do any abilities associated with that usage resolve (such as \xe2\x80\x9cReap:\xe2\x80\x9d or \xe2\x80\x9cFight:\xe2\x80\x9d abilities). Therefore, when using Anger on a stunned creature, the creature would become exhausted, the stun token would be removed, and no fight or \xe2\x80\x9cFight:\xe2\x80\x9d abilities would resolve, regardless of the active house or circumstances that may prevent the creature from being used to fight normally.
